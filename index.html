Course Learning Reflections

Each team member will provide the following in separate pages. Each student will logically divide sections and sub-sections as per their understanding:

  • What are the kinds of problems we see in nature? (iteration, recursion, backtracking)
  • What is space and time efficiency? Why are they important? Explain different classes of problems and orders of growth.
  • Key takeaways from different design principles from Chapter 2.
  • The hierarchical data and how different tree data structures solve and optimize over problem scenarios (tree, BST, AVL, 2-3, red-black, heap, trie).
  • The need for array query algorithms and their implications. Applications and principles need discussion.
  • Differentiate between tree and graph traversals, their applications, and real-world connection.
  • Deliberate on sorting and searching algorithms, techniques, and real-world connections.

  • What strategies do you use to identify patterns in datasets or problems?
  • How do you decide when to prioritize simplicity over optimization?
  • Reflect on breaking down problems into smaller components to approach them effectively.
